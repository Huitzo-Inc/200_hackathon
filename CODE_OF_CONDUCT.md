# Huitzo Hackathon 2026 - Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We are committed to providing a welcoming, inclusive, and harassment-free experience for everyone participating in the Huitzo Hackathon 2026, regardless of age, body size, disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming, diverse, inclusive, and healthy community.

## Our Standards

### Positive Behavior

Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment include:

* **Being respectful and constructive** in all interactions
* **Welcoming newcomers** and helping them get started with Huitzo
* **Sharing knowledge generously** - we all benefit when we learn together
* **Giving and gracefully accepting constructive feedback**
* **Focusing on what's best** for the community and the hackathon
* **Showing empathy** toward other community members
* **Celebrating others' successes** and learning from their approaches
* **Being patient** when helping others troubleshoot issues

### Unacceptable Behavior

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

* **Harassment** of any kind, including offensive comments related to personal characteristics
* **Trolling, insulting, or derogatory comments**, and personal or political attacks
* **Public or private harassment**
* **Publishing others' private information** (doxxing) without explicit permission
* **Plagiarism** - submitting others' work as your own
* **Cheating** - violating hackathon rules or gaming the competition unfairly
* **Spamming** Discord channels, GitHub issues, or office hours
* **Intentionally disrupting** community spaces or hackathon activities
* **Other conduct** which could reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional setting

## Hackathon-Specific Guidelines

### Fair Competition

* **Original work**: Your submission must be your own work created during the hackathon period
* **Open source spirit**: Share knowledge, but don't copy submissions
* **Bug bounty integrity**: Report bugs honestly; don't exploit them maliciously
* **Collaboration is encouraged**: Team up, help each other, but submit your own unique packs

### Community Spaces

This Code of Conduct applies to all hackathon spaces, including:

* **GitHub repositories** (issues, pull requests, discussions)
* **Discord server** (all channels and DMs)
* **Office hours** (in-person or virtual)
* **Email communications** with organizers and participants
* **Social media** when representing the hackathon or Huitzo

## Enforcement

### Reporting

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, please report it by:

* **Email**: ernesto@huitzo.ai
* **Discord**: Contact a moderator or admin
* **GitHub**: Flag inappropriate content or contact maintainers

All reports will be handled with discretion and confidentiality.

### Consequences

Organizers may take any action they deem appropriate, including:

1. **Warning** - A private or public warning about the behavior
2. **Temporary ban** - Temporary suspension from community spaces
3. **Permanent ban** - Permanent removal from the hackathon and community
4. **Disqualification** - Removal from prize consideration

### Appeals

If you believe you've been unfairly sanctioned, you may appeal by emailing ernesto@huitzo.ai with a detailed explanation.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies during the hackathon period (February 17-24, 2026) and afterwards when an individual is representing the project or community.
